We utilize other means of passively collecting information:

“Cookies”. Cookies are bits of electronic information that a website can transfer to a user's hard drive to keep records of his or her visit at the website. We may use cookies to improve your experiences when visiting the Site, and to anonymously track interest in, and collect aggregate information on, our Site.

“IDFA”. IDFA is a tag or identifier which applies to your device(s) that utilize(s) Apple’s iOS. We use the IDFA to customize our advertising to your preferences. You may reset the IDFA at any time and opt out of all remarketing by enabling a device setting called “Limit Ad Tracking” (LAT), which is designed to restrict advertisers from using the IDFA for behavioral advertising. Learn more about how to limit ad tracking on your iOS device or how to reset your IDFA by clicking here: IDFA Opt-Out.

“Log files” are files that log actions that have occurred on a website. PF may use log files to gather statistics about your browsing habits and to assess overall Site activity, including how many "hits" a web page receives. Log entries are generated anonymously, are not associated with any particular user, computer, or browser, and enable us to track interest promotions, troubleshoot technical concerns, and provide users with content that may be of interest to them. We also use this information for internal purposes to improve the App and the Site. “Pixel Tags” are transparent GIF images that are added to a web page. We use pixels on our Site to measure and understand our users so that we can customize our advertising. “Tags” or “Container Tags”. Tags are little bits of code. We use Tags on our Site and in our App and on our Site to help us to measure impressions across platforms in order to analyze the responsiveness of our advertising campaigns and to better understand how you use and enjoy the Site and the App.

“Web beacons”. Web beacons are small pieces of data that are embedded in images on the pages of websites. We use web beacons to analyze traffic patterns on our Site, such as the frequency with which users visit various pages of our Site. These technical methods may involve the transmission of data to us or to another party authorized by us to collect information on our behalf. We also use track HTML emails that we send so that we may understand whether users have opened the emails or clicked on links contained in the emails. These technical methods may collect information in a form that is personally identifiable.

Information collected via the Site and the App may be stored in the United States, and retained until the purpose of collection or use or mandated retention period expires.
